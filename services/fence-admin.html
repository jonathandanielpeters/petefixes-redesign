Chainlink Pricing

Master material list + build recipes. The fence drawing tool uses these recipes to calculate the parts needed for each post type, per foot of fence, and per gate type. Color variants (galvanized/black) are matched automatically to the customer's selection.

Materials — Master List
One card per material type (Tension Band, Post, Brace Band, etc.). Each card shows a cost matrix of size × color variants. Change the markup once at the top of a card and the sell price for all variants updates automatically. Sell Price = Cost × (1 + Markup/100).
Master markup: %
Build Recipes — Parts list per component
For each type of post (end, corner, line), per linear foot of fence, and per gate type, define which materials are used and at what quantity. Use "By Height" when the quantity changes for 4/5/6 ft fences (e.g. tension bands). Color is matched automatically to the customer's fence color.
Note: This pricing engine is the new chainlink system. Your fence tool will be wired to use it in the next update. Until then, the existing fence tool continues to use the legacy pricing fields (preserved below this section for safety).
End Post Assembly
Parts needed per end post (where a fence run terminates). Multiplied by the number of end posts in the customer's drawing.
Corner Post Assembly
Parts needed per corner post (two fence runs meeting at an angle). Multiplied by the number of corner posts.
Line Post Assembly
Parts needed per line post (interior posts along a straight run). Multiplied by the number of line posts.
Per Linear Foot of Fence
Materials applied per foot of fence length (e.g. fabric at 1/ft, top rail at 0.1/ft = one 10ft rail per 10 ft, fence ties at 1/ft).
Gate Hardware — By Gate Type
Different gate types need different hardware attachments. Each gate in the customer's drawing pulls the parts list for its type.

⚡Labour
Hourly rates, break time, overtime rules, travel, and per-task time estimates. These feed into the Sample Fence Prices below and the customer-facing quotes.
Hourly Rates & Break Time
What you pay labour vs. what you charge the customer. Break minutes per workday (coffee breaks + lunch) scale with job size based on hours worked.
Profit Target
Target profit per full workday — Pete's baseline income goal. Each job should contribute its share based on the hours it consumes. A job that fills 50% of a day should hit 50% of the target (assuming another job fills the other half).
Per-job target = (job hours ÷ hours per workday) × target profit / workday
Overtime
Overtime threshold + multipliers, plus the maximum hours one worker can be scheduled per day. Jobs needing more than the max auto-split across multiple days — each day gets its own regular + overtime hours.
Travel & Setup
Shop location + travel/load-up time. Travel time is round-trip; cost is multiplied by how many employees on the job.
Task Times
For each task in a job, how many minutes it takes per unit and how many employees are involved. Unit: per job = once, per line = × distinct fence line segment, per post = × total post count, per foot = × linear footage, per hookup = × end+corner posts, per gate = × gate count. Add your own tasks at the bottom.
