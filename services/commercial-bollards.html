What Are Bollards

The frontline of
perimeter protection.

Bollards are short, sturdy vertical posts designed to control vehicle traffic and protect people, buildings and infrastructure from accidental or intentional vehicle impact. They are one of the most effective perimeter security measures available, creating a physical barrier between vehicles and the areas you need to protect — storefronts, pedestrian walkways, building entrances, utility equipment and outdoor dining patios.

In Winnipeg, bollards have become increasingly important for commercial properties. Ram-raid attacks on retail storefronts, vehicle incursions into pedestrian zones and accidental impacts in busy parking lots are real risks that bollards mitigate. Pete Fixes installs bollards across the full security spectrum, from basic parking lot guides to crash-rated security posts that can stop a loaded truck at speed.

  • Storefront and building entrance protection
  • Pedestrian walkway and patio safety
  • Parking lot traffic control
  • Ram-raid and vehicle incursion prevention
  • Utility and equipment protection

Fixed vs Removable

Permanent protection
or flexible access.

Fixed bollards are permanently set in concrete foundations and provide the highest level of vehicle resistance. They are ideal for locations where the barrier needs to be permanent — building frontages, utility pad perimeters and pedestrian zone boundaries. Our fixed bollards are installed with deep concrete footings engineered for Manitoba's frost conditions, ensuring they remain plumb and secure through years of freeze-thaw cycles.

Removable bollards use a receiver sleeve set flush in the ground, allowing the bollard post to be lifted out when vehicle access is needed and replaced when it is not. This is the right choice for loading zones, emergency access routes, seasonal patios and event spaces that need to switch between protected and open configurations. Our removable systems use locking pins to prevent unauthorized removal and stainless steel receivers that resist corrosion from road salt.

  • Fixed bollards with deep concrete footings
  • Removable bollards with flush-mount receivers
  • Locking pin systems to prevent tampering
  • Stainless steel receivers resist road salt
  • Frost-rated foundations for Manitoba climate
  • Retractable options for high-traffic zones

Decorative vs Security-Rated

Matching aesthetics
with protection level.

Not every bollard needs to stop a vehicle at highway speed. Decorative bollards are designed primarily for traffic guidance, property delineation and architectural enhancement. Available in cast iron, stainless steel, aluminum and powder-coated steel, decorative bollards can be matched to your building's design language — from heritage styles that suit Winnipeg's Exchange District to sleek modern profiles for new developments.

When genuine vehicle-stopping power is required, Pete Fixes installs security-rated bollards tested to ASTM F2656 and PAS 68 standards. These crash-rated bollards use thick-wall steel pipe, reinforced base plates and deep concrete foundations engineered to arrest vehicles at specific speeds and weights. We help you determine the appropriate security rating based on your threat assessment, site layout and insurance or regulatory requirements.

  • Decorative cast iron, steel and aluminum
  • Heritage and modern design profiles
  • ASTM F2656 crash-rated security bollards
  • PAS 68 tested vehicle arrest systems
  • Custom powder-coat colour matching
  • Integrated lighting options available

Installation Process

Efficient installation
with minimal disruption.

Pete Fixes manages the entire bollard installation process from site survey through completion. We begin with an on-site assessment to evaluate your perimeter needs, identify underground utilities using Manitoba Hydro's Click Before You Dig service, and determine the optimal bollard spacing and placement. Our team prepares detailed layout drawings for your approval before any work begins.

Installation involves core drilling or excavation for each bollard location, setting the concrete foundations and aligning the bollard posts to ensure a clean, uniform appearance. For existing concrete or asphalt surfaces, we use core drilling to minimize disruption to your property. Most commercial bollard installations are completed in one to two days depending on the number of units and site conditions. We coordinate work to minimize impact on your business operations, scheduling around peak hours when needed.

  • Free on-site survey and layout planning
  • Underground utility locating included
  • Core drilling for existing surfaces
  • 1-2 day installation for most projects
  • Minimal disruption to business operations
