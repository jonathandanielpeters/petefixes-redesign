How to Secure Your Winnipeg Property: Fencing, Gates & Smart Technology

April 5, 2026 · 12 min read

Winnipeg consistently ranks among the highest cities in Canada for property crime. Vehicle theft, garage break-ins, and back lane prowling are realities that homeowners and business operators deal with year-round. And most of the time, the intruder's path leads through the weakest point on the property — an unlocked gate, a sagging fence section, or a gap where the fence meets the garage.

The good news is that property security is solvable. It doesn't require a single expensive product or a dramatic overhaul. The most effective approach is layered: start with a strong physical perimeter, control who passes through it, and add smart technology to detect and respond to threats. Each layer reinforces the others, and you can build them over time as your budget allows.

This guide walks through every layer of property security available to Winnipeg homeowners and businesses — from the fence line to the phone in your pocket.

Layer 1: The Fence — Your Perimeter

A perimeter fence is the foundation of any security plan. It defines your property boundary, creates a physical barrier to entry, and sends a clear signal that your property is not an easy target. Without a solid fence, cameras and alarms are just documenting break-ins that have already happened.

Why the Fence Matters More Than You Think

Most opportunistic property crime in Winnipeg follows a pattern: someone walks or drives through a back lane, tests a few gates, and enters the yard that offers the least resistance. A fence in good condition with a secure gate eliminates your property from that shortlist. It doesn't need to be impenetrable — it just needs to be more effort than the next yard down the lane.

For properties that face targeted threats — commercial yards, equipment storage, fleet compounds — the fence needs to do more than discourage. It needs to resist cutting, climbing, and forced entry. That's where material choice and design become critical.

Anti-Climb and Cut-Proof Fencing Options

Not all fences provide the same level of security. Here are the options ranked from standard deterrent to high-security, all of which we install in Winnipeg:

  • Standard 6-foot chain link: Adequate for residential perimeter security. Deters casual entry but can be climbed or cut with basic tools. A good starting point that can be upgraded later.
  • 8-foot commercial chain link with barbed wire or razor wire: The standard for commercial and industrial properties. The added height and top deterrent make climbing significantly harder. Razor wire is restricted to commercial zones in Winnipeg — check local fencing regulations before installing.
  • Ornamental steel with no horizontal rails: Vertical pickets with no horizontal members eliminate footholds. This is an excellent residential option that combines security with curb appeal. Difficult to climb and nearly impossible to bend or force through.
  • Cut-proof welded mesh panels: Heavy-gauge steel mesh with tight apertures (typically 3-inch by 0.5-inch openings) that resists bolt cutters and cannot be climbed. This is the standard for high-security applications — data centres, utility substations, and high-value storage yards.

Weak Spots to Address

Even a well-built fence has vulnerabilities. In Winnipeg, these are the most common weak points we see during security assessments:

  • Gates: The gate is almost always the weakest link. A strong fence with a flimsy gate is like a vault with a screen door. Gates need heavy-duty latches, locks, and ideally automation (more on this in Layer 2).
  • Old or damaged sections: A single rotted post or sagging section creates an entry point. Frost heave, age, and vehicle impacts all take their toll on Winnipeg fences. If part of your fence is compromised, address it before adding cameras or technology. Our fence repair guide covers common fixes.
  • Gaps at the bottom: Ground erosion, uneven terrain, and frost heave can create gaps under the fence large enough to crawl through. A bottom rail, tension wire, or gravel fill closes these gaps.
  • Where the fence meets structures: The junction between your fence and your garage, shed, or house is a common bypass point. Make sure there's no gap and that the connection is secured with hardware, not just friction.

Emergency Fence Repair

When a vehicle backs through your fence at 2 a.m., or a storm takes down a section overnight, your property is suddenly exposed. In Winnipeg, where back lanes see regular traffic and overnight temperatures can make temporary fixes difficult, getting that perimeter closed up matters.

We offer emergency fence repair for situations where damage leaves your property unsecured. A temporary chain link panel or barricade can be installed same-day to restore the perimeter while permanent repair is scheduled. If your fence is down and your yard is open, call 204-894-9071.

Layer 2: The Gate — Controlled Access

The gate is the only intentional opening in your perimeter, which makes it both the most important and most vulnerable point. An unlocked or easily opened gate defeats the purpose of every foot of fence around it. Electric gates and controlled-access pedestrian gates turn this weakness into a strength.

Electric Vehicle Gates

An electric gate eliminates the most common residential security failure: the gate that gets left open. Whether it's a slide gate on a back lane or a swing gate on a driveway, an electric operator ensures the gate closes and locks automatically after every use.

For vehicle access, the gate can be triggered by:

  • Remote control: A handheld transmitter in your vehicle — press the button as you approach.
  • Keypad or fob: Enter a code or tap a fob at a post-mounted reader. Useful for properties with multiple authorized drivers.
  • Licence plate reader cameras: The camera reads your plate as you approach and opens the gate automatically if your plate is in the authorized database. Fully hands-free, works day and night, and logs every entry.
  • Smartphone app: Open your gate from your phone, from anywhere. Useful for letting in deliveries or contractors when you're away.

Pedestrian Gates and Door Access

Side gates, pedestrian entrances, and building doors need their own layer of control. An electric door lock paired with a keypad fob reader means no one walks through without authorization. For multi-tenant properties or commercial buildings, an access control system manages who can enter which doors, at what times, and logs every event.

Pedestrian gate options include:

  • Magnetic locks with fob readers: The gate stays locked at all times and releases when an authorized fob is tapped. Fobs can be deactivated instantly if lost.
  • Keypad entry: A PIN code unlocks the gate. Codes can be changed easily and different codes assigned to different people for tracking.
  • Video intercom: Visitors press a button, you see and speak with them on your phone, and you decide whether to unlock the gate remotely.

Emergency Gate Repair

A gate stuck in the open position is an immediate security risk. Operator failures, power outages, damaged tracks, and broken hinges can all leave your gate unable to close. In winter, ice buildup on slide gate tracks is a common cause of gate failure in Winnipeg.

We provide emergency gate repair to get your gate closed and operational. If your gate is stuck open and you can't secure it manually, contact us for priority service.

Layer 3: Smart Technology — Eyes and Ears

Once your physical perimeter is solid and your gates are controlled, technology extends your awareness beyond what fences and locks can do alone. Modern security technology doesn't just record — it detects, identifies, and alerts in real time.

AI Facial Recognition Cameras

Facial recognition cameras identify known versus unknown individuals as they approach your property. The system maintains a database of authorized faces (family, employees, regular visitors) and flags anyone it doesn't recognize. Alerts are sent to your phone immediately, with a photo and timestamp.

This technology is particularly valuable for commercial properties where you need to know not just that someone entered, but exactly who it was and when. It also creates a searchable log — if an incident occurs, you can pull up every appearance of a specific individual across all your cameras.

Outdoor Tracking Cameras

Standard security cameras record a fixed view. Outdoor tracking cameras go further — they detect movement and physically follow a person or vehicle across your property with a motorized pan-tilt-zoom lens. If someone enters your yard and moves toward your garage, the camera tracks them the entire way, keeping them in frame and recording continuous footage.

This eliminates the blind spots that fixed cameras leave between their fields of view. For larger properties, tracking cameras can cover areas that would otherwise require three or four fixed cameras.

High-Security Camera Monitoring

Cameras that record are useful after a crime. Cameras that are monitored in real time can prevent one. Professional monitoring services watch your camera feeds around the clock. When an alert triggers — motion detected after hours, a person in a restricted area, a vehicle in the back lane — a trained operator reviews the feed and takes action. That action might be activating a two-way speaker to warn the intruder, calling the property owner, or dispatching police.

For high-value commercial properties, fleet yards, and construction sites, professional monitoring turns a passive camera system into an active security response.

Fence Alarm Systems

A fence alarm detects physical interaction with the fence itself — climbing, cutting, or impact. Sensors mounted along the fence line pick up vibrations and distinguish between security events (someone shaking the fence or using a cutting tool) and false alarms (wind, animals, or a ball hitting the fence).

When the fence alarm triggers, it can activate cameras to record the specific section, send alerts to your phone, trigger floodlights, or notify a monitoring station. Combined with your perimeter fence, this creates an early warning system that detects intrusion attempts before the intruder gets inside.

Vehicle Detection

In-ground inductive loops or above-ground radar sensors detect vehicles approaching your gate or entering your property. They serve multiple purposes: triggering the gate to open for authorized vehicles, preventing the gate from closing on a vehicle in the opening, and alerting you when a vehicle enters your driveway or back lane approach after hours.

For commercial parking lots and gated compounds, vehicle detection loops pair with licence plate reader cameras to create a fully automated entry system — the loop confirms a vehicle is present, the LPR reads the plate, and the gate opens if authorized.

Solar-Powered Cameras

Not every part of your property has power access. Back lane fences, detached garages, remote storage yards, and construction sites often lack convenient electrical connections. Solar-powered cameras with cellular connectivity solve this problem — they generate their own power, store footage locally or in the cloud, and transmit alerts over cellular networks.

In Winnipeg, solar cameras need appropriately sized panels to account for shorter winter days and snow coverage. A well-designed solar camera installation includes a panel large enough to charge through December and January, plus battery storage for several days of cloudy weather. These systems work year-round when properly specified.

Layer 4: Access Control Integration

Individual security components — a fence, a gate operator, cameras, door locks — are effective on their own. But when they're integrated through an access control system, they become dramatically more powerful.

How Integration Works

An access control panel acts as the brain that connects every security component on your property. When someone taps a fob at the pedestrian gate, the panel checks authorization, unlocks the gate, records the event with timestamp and user ID, and triggers the nearest camera to capture a photo. All of this happens in under a second.

Integration means your systems talk to each other:

  • Fence alarm triggers camera: When the fence sensor detects climbing, cameras automatically point to that section and begin recording.
  • LPR triggers gate: The licence plate reader identifies an authorized vehicle and sends the open command to the gate operator.
  • After-hours lockdown: At a scheduled time, the system automatically locks all pedestrian gates, arms fence alarms, and switches cameras to motion-detection recording.
  • Single dashboard: View live camera feeds, access logs, alarm history, and gate status from one interface — on a desktop, tablet, or phone.

Remote Monitoring and Alerts

Modern access control software sends push notifications to your phone for any event you choose to monitor: gate opened after hours, unrecognized face detected, fence alarm triggered, door held open too long. You can view live camera feeds, unlock gates remotely, and review access logs from anywhere.

For businesses, the software generates reports — who accessed which areas, when, and how often. This data is valuable for insurance documentation, compliance, employee management, and incident investigation. Cost vs. Security Level

Security doesn't have to be all-or-nothing. Here's how the investment scales with the level of protection, based on a typical Winnipeg residential or small commercial property:

Security Level What's Included Approximate Cost
Basic 6-foot perimeter fence, locking manual gate, motion-activated lights $4,000 – $10,000
Mid-Range Perimeter fence, electric gate with fob/keypad, 4–6 security cameras with recording $10,000 – $20,000
High-Security Anti-climb fencing, electric gate with LPR, AI cameras with tracking, fence alarm, access control panel, professional monitoring $20,000 – $50,000+

Most residential properties in Winnipeg land in the basic to mid-range category. The high-security tier is typically reserved for commercial yards, multi-unit residential complexes, fleet compounds, and properties that have experienced repeated incidents.

The layered approach means you don't have to spend everything at once. Start with the fence and gate — the physical foundation. Add cameras when budget allows. Integrate with access control when you're ready to tie it all together. Each layer adds value on its own and multiplies the effectiveness of the layers already in place.

Winter Security in Winnipeg Winnipeg's winter introduces unique security challenges. Snowdrifts against fences create climbable ramps. Ice buildup can disable gate operators. Shorter daylight hours mean more hours of darkness for opportunistic crime. When planning your security system, account for winter: choose heated camera housings, ice-resistant gate hardware, and ensure your fence design doesn't collect drifts that reduce its effective height.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most break-in resistant fence for a Winnipeg property?

Cut-proof welded mesh panels are the most break-in resistant option. Made from heavy-gauge steel with small apertures, they resist bolt cutters and cannot be climbed easily. For residential properties, a 6-foot ornamental fence with no horizontal rails (which act as footholds) paired with security cameras offers excellent protection without looking industrial.

How much does a full property security system cost in Winnipeg?

A basic perimeter fence with a manual gate runs $4,000 to $10,000 depending on property size and materials. Adding an electric gate operator costs $2,500 to $6,000. A camera system with 4 to 8 cameras runs $2,000 to $8,000. Full integration with access control software, licence plate readers, and professional monitoring can bring the total to $20,000 or more for comprehensive commercial-grade security.

Can I get emergency fence or gate repair in Winnipeg?

Yes. If your fence or gate is damaged and leaving your property exposed, Pete Fixes offers emergency repair service. A vehicle through a fence, a gate stuck open, or storm damage that compromises your perimeter are all situations where same-day or next-day repair is available. Call 204-894-9071 for emergency service.

Do security cameras work in Winnipeg winters?

Modern outdoor security cameras are rated for temperatures as low as -40°C and work reliably through Winnipeg winters. Look for cameras with heated housings to prevent lens fogging and ice buildup. Solar-powered cameras may produce less power during short winter days, so a larger solar panel or battery backup is recommended for year-round solar installations.
