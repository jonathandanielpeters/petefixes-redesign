License Plate Readers vs Fob Systems vs Intercoms: Choosing Access Control for Your Winnipeg Property

April 5, 2026 · 10 min read

Whether you're managing a condo building, a commercial parking lot, a gated community, or securing your own property, access control is the backbone of modern security. But with so many technologies available — from traditional fob systems to AI-powered cameras — choosing the right system can be overwhelming.

This guide compares every major access control system available for Winnipeg properties, with honest pros, cons, and cost ranges to help you decide.

Access Control Systems at a Glance

Key Fob and Proximity Card Systems

The fob system is the workhorse of access control. Users carry a small key fob or proximity card and tap it against a reader to unlock doors, gates, or barriers. It's reliable, affordable, and well-understood.

How it works: Each fob has a unique ID. The access control panel stores a database of authorized fob IDs. When a fob is tapped, the panel checks the database and grants or denies access. Fobs can be programmed with time restrictions (e.g., business hours only), zone restrictions (e.g., parking but not building), and can be deactivated instantly if lost or stolen.

Best for: Apartment buildings, office buildings, parking garages, and gated communities. A keypad fob for door systems (combined keypad + fob reader) gives you dual-factor security — users need both the fob and a PIN code.

Winnipeg considerations: Fob readers work well in cold weather. Choose weatherproof, heated readers for outdoor installations. Proximity cards in wallets can crack in extreme cold — key fobs are more durable for outdoor gate use.

Swipe Card Systems

A swipe card system works similarly to fobs but uses magnetic stripe cards that are swiped through a reader. They're common in older buildings and offices but are gradually being replaced by proximity (tap) technology.

Swipe cards are cheaper to produce than fobs (useful for high-turnover environments like hotels or large workplaces) but the readers have moving parts that wear out and can jam in cold weather. For new installations in Winnipeg, we generally recommend proximity systems over magnetic swipe.

License Plate Reader (LPR) Cameras

License plate reader cameras automatically capture and read the plate number of every vehicle approaching a gate, barrier, or parking entrance. Authorized plates trigger the gate to open — the driver never has to stop, roll down the window, or tap anything.

How it works: Specialized cameras with infrared illumination capture plate images day and night. Software reads the plate number, checks it against a database, and triggers the gate operator if authorized. All entries are logged with timestamp, plate image, and vehicle photo.

Best for: Commercial parking lots, condo parking, fleet yards, and any vehicle-access point where hands-free, high-throughput operation is needed. LPR also creates an automatic log of every vehicle that enters and exits — valuable for security and dispute resolution.

Winnipeg considerations: LPR cameras must handle snow, ice, and dirt on plates. Modern LPR systems use IR illumination and AI processing to read plates even when partially obscured. Camera positioning matters — salt spray from vehicles can coat the lens, so regular cleaning or heated camera housings are recommended.

Building Intercom and Door Systems

A building intercom door system allows visitors to call a resident or office from the building entrance. The resident can see and speak with the visitor (via video intercom) and remotely unlock the door or gate.

Modern intercom systems connect to smartphones — visitors call through the intercom panel, and the resident answers on their phone from anywhere. This is especially useful for package deliveries, contractors, and visitors when you're not home.

Intercoms integrate well with electric door locks and access control panels. A resident can buzz in a visitor, and the access control system logs the entry with timestamp and unit number.

AI Facial Recognition and Smart Cameras

AI facial recognition cameras represent the newest generation of access control. Instead of fobs, cards, or keypads, the system identifies authorized people by their face and grants access automatically.

Practical applications:

  • High-security facilities where credentials can be stolen or shared
  • Staff entrances at commercial properties
  • VIP areas and restricted zones
  • Integration with high-security camera monitoring systems for real-time alerts on unrecognized individuals

AI camera systems go beyond access control. Outdoor tracking cameras can follow individuals across a property, detect loitering or unusual behaviour, and send real-time alerts to security personnel or property owners via smartphone.

Privacy Note Facial recognition systems are subject to privacy laws in Manitoba and Canada. Make sure your installation complies with PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) and any applicable provincial regulations. Visible signage notifying people of camera surveillance is required.

Vehicle Detection Systems

Vehicle detection systems use in-ground inductive loops, above-ground radar sensors, or camera-based detection to sense when a vehicle is present. They're used to:

  • Trigger automatic gates to open when an authorized vehicle approaches
  • Prevent gates from closing on vehicles still in the opening
  • Count vehicles in parking lots for capacity management
  • Trigger cameras to record when a vehicle enters or exits

Vehicle detection loops are often paired with other access control methods. For example, an LPR camera reads the plate while the detection loop confirms a vehicle is actually present (preventing false triggers from pedestrians or debris).

Keypad and PIN Code Systems

The simplest form of access control: a numeric keypad where users enter a code to unlock a door or gate. Low cost, no credentials to manage, and easy to change codes when needed.

Keypads work well for pedestrian gates, side doors, storage units, and low-traffic access points. For higher security, combine a keypad with a fob reader for two-factor authentication. Weatherproof keypads with backlit buttons are essential for Winnipeg's outdoor installations.

Perimeter Security: Fence Alarms and Monitoring

Access control doesn't stop at the gate. For high-security properties, fence alarm systems detect cutting, climbing, or impacts on the perimeter fence. These systems use vibration sensors, taut wire, or fibre optic cable mounted along the fence line to detect intrusion attempts.

Combined with outdoor tracking cameras and high-security camera monitoring, a fence alarm system provides complete perimeter awareness. When the fence sensor triggers, cameras automatically point to the intrusion point and begin recording, while alerts are sent to the property owner or monitoring station.

Solar-Powered Security Options

For remote locations, back lanes, or properties without easy electrical access, solar-powered cameras and solar-powered gate operators eliminate the need for electrical infrastructure. Modern solar security cameras store enough charge for days of cloudy weather and include cellular connectivity for remote monitoring.

Access Control Software and Integration

Modern access control software ties all your security systems together — fobs, cameras, intercoms, LPR, and alarms — into a single dashboard. Access control software integrations allow you to:

  • Manage all users and credentials from one interface
  • Set schedules, zones, and access levels
  • View real-time camera feeds alongside access logs
  • Receive push notifications for security events
  • Generate reports for compliance, insurance, or management
  • Integrate with building management systems, elevator controls, and fire safety

How to Secure Your Property: Layered Approach

The best security isn't a single device — it's layers. Here's how we recommend securing a property in Winnipeg:

  1. Perimeter: Quality fencing (chain link, ornamental, or cut-proof fencing for high-security) with fence alarm if needed
  2. Entry points: Automatic gates with access control (fob, LPR, keypad, or intercom)
  3. Surveillance: Camera system covering entry points, parking areas, and building perimeter
  4. Doors: Electric door locks with fob or smartphone access
  5. Monitoring: Recorded footage with AI-powered alerts for unusual activity
