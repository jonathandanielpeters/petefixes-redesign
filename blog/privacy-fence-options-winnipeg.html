Privacy Fence Options in Winnipeg: From Budget Screens to Soundproof Walls (2026)

April 5, 2026 · 11 min read

Privacy is the number-one reason Winnipeg homeowners build fences. But "privacy fence" means very different things depending on who you ask. For one homeowner it's a few dollars' worth of slats clipped into existing chain link. For another it's a full concrete sound barrier blocking highway noise. The budget range is just as wide — from under $5 per foot to well over $100.

This guide walks through every privacy fence option available in Winnipeg, organized from cheapest to most expensive, so you can find the right balance of privacy, cost, and durability for your property. If you're looking for general fence pricing first, our Winnipeg fence cost guide covers all fence types and materials.

Budget Privacy Options (Under $30/ft)

You don't need to spend thousands to get meaningful privacy. These options work well for renters, seasonal use, or homeowners who already have a fence and want to add screening without starting over.

Chain Link Privacy Inserts (Slats)

If you already have a chain link fence, privacy inserts are the cheapest way to block sightlines. Slats weave vertically through the chain link mesh, turning a see-through fence into one that blocks roughly 70-90% of visibility depending on the style.

There are three main types:

  • Winged slats — rigid PVC strips that lock into each diamond in the mesh. The most common and durable option. They provide about 75% privacy and hold up well in wind.
  • Hedge-style inserts — artificial hedge strips that thread through the chain link, creating a green wall look. These offer around 90% privacy and soften the industrial appearance of chain link.
  • Fabric/mesh screens — woven polyethylene fabric that attaches to the fence with zip ties or grommets. The cheapest option but also the least durable in Winnipeg's wind.

Expect to pay $5-8 per foot for slat inserts (materials only). Installation is straightforward enough for a weekend DIY project — you're essentially weaving strips through the mesh by hand. A 100-foot fence takes most people about 3-4 hours. For a full breakdown of chain link fence costs, see our pricing guide.

Canvas and Fabric Privacy Screens

Temporary fabric screens are the most affordable privacy option at $3-5 per foot. They attach to any existing fence, railing, or post with zip ties, grommets, or bungee cords. They're popular for patios, balconies, construction sites, and event areas where permanent fencing isn't practical.

The downside is durability. Winnipeg's prairie winds are relentless, and fabric screens act like sails. Budget screens rarely survive more than one or two seasons before tearing or pulling free from their fasteners. If you go this route, use heavy-duty construction-grade mesh (the kind with reinforced edges and brass grommets) rather than the thin screens sold for residential patios.

Tree and Hedge Fencing

A row of cedars or willows is the most natural privacy screen you can plant. It's also the slowest — most hedge species take 3-7 years to fill in enough to block sightlines, and Winnipeg's short growing season doesn't help.

Winnipeg-hardy species for living privacy screens include:

  • Brandon pyramidal cedar — the most popular hedge tree in Manitoba. Grows about 30 cm per year and tops out at 4-5 metres. Dense enough to block wind and views once established.
  • Hybrid willow — grows fast (1-2 metres per year) but requires more maintenance and takes up more yard space. Good for acreages or large lots.
  • Colorado spruce — slow-growing but extremely dense. A mature row of spruce is nearly soundproof. Works well as a windbreak on exposed lots.
  • Lilac hedge — deciduous, so you only get summer privacy. But lilacs are tough, fragrant, and well-suited to Winnipeg's climate.

Planting costs range from $15-50 per tree depending on species and size. A 100-foot hedge of Brandon cedars spaced 3 feet apart (about 33 trees at $25 each) costs around $800 in plants alone. The tradeoff is time — this isn't instant privacy.

Pressure-Treated Wood Privacy Fence

The standard privacy fence in Winnipeg is a 6-foot pressure-treated wood fence with vertical 1x6 boards. It's the go-to for homeowners who want full, immediate privacy at the most affordable installed price: $28-36 per foot.

A typical 150-foot backyard perimeter in pressure-treated wood runs $4,200-$5,400 installed, including posts, rails, boards, and one gate. The boards are placed side by side with minimal gaps, giving you close to 100% visual privacy from day one. For more on wood fence options, visit our residential wood fencing page.

The trade-off is maintenance. Pressure-treated wood needs staining or sealing every 2-3 years to prevent greying and surface deterioration. Our cedar fence maintenance guide covers staining techniques that apply to pressure-treated lumber as well.

Mid-Range Privacy Options ($30-50/ft)

Stepping up in budget gets you better materials, tighter construction, and less long-term upkeep. These options are where most Winnipeg homeowners land when they want a fence that looks good and lasts.

Board-on-Board Fence

A standard wood privacy fence places boards side by side. Over time, as the wood shrinks and shifts, small gaps appear between boards. A board-on-board fence solves this by overlapping adjacent boards — each board covers the gap between the two behind it.

The result is a fence with zero gaps from any viewing angle. Even as the wood shrinks seasonally, the overlap keeps sightlines completely blocked. Board-on-board fences also look the same from both sides, which avoids the "good side / bad side" problem that can cause friction with neighbours.

The cost increase is modest — about $4-6 per foot more than a standard side-by-side fence — because you're using roughly 30% more boards. Expect $34-42 per foot installed for pressure-treated board-on-board, and $45-55 per foot in cedar.

Cedar Privacy Fence

Cedar is the premium wood choice for privacy fencing in Winnipeg. It's naturally resistant to rot, insects, and moisture — qualities that matter in a climate where fences endure -35C winters, spring thaws, and summer humidity all in the same year.

A 6-foot cedar privacy fence runs $40-50 per foot installed. The colour starts as a warm reddish-brown and ages to silver-grey over 2-3 years if left untreated. Many homeowners prefer the natural grey patina. If you'd rather keep the warm tone, staining every 2-3 years will do it — see our cedar maintenance guide for timing and product recommendations specific to Manitoba.

Cedar costs more upfront than pressure-treated wood, but the natural rot resistance means the boards last longer before needing replacement. Most cedar fences in Winnipeg last 20-25 years with basic maintenance.

Vinyl Privacy Fence

Vinyl fencing has grown in popularity across Winnipeg over the past decade, and for good reason: it never needs staining, painting, or sealing. A vinyl privacy fence looks the same in year 15 as it did in year one — just rinse it with a hose.

Modern vinyl privacy fences run $40-55 per foot installed. They come in white, tan, grey, and wood-grain finishes. Most quality vinyl fence systems use steel or aluminum reinforcements inside the posts and rails, which is essential in Winnipeg. Without metal reinforcement, vinyl posts can flex and eventually snap in sustained prairie winds or heavy snow loads.

The main consideration for Winnipeg homeowners is cold-weather brittleness. Cheap, thin vinyl can crack if struck hard in extreme cold. Commercial-grade vinyl (thicker walls, UV stabilizers, impact modifiers) handles Manitoba winters without issue. Ask your installer about the wall thickness and impact rating — you want fence-grade vinyl, not the thin stuff designed for milder climates. Learn more on our vinyl fencing page.

Composite Fencing

Composite fencing is made from a blend of wood fibres and plastic polymers — essentially the same technology as composite decking. The result is a fence that looks and feels like wood but requires almost no maintenance.

Composite fence panels typically cost $40-55 per foot installed. They won't rot, warp, split, or attract insects, and they don't need staining. The colour is embedded throughout the material rather than applied to the surface, so it resists fading.

Composite panels are usually sold as complete systems — posts, rails, and infill boards designed to fit together. This makes for a clean, consistent appearance but limits customization. You're choosing from the manufacturer's available heights, colours, and styles rather than building from individual boards. Popular brands available in Manitoba include Trex, SimTek, and Fiberon.

Premium Privacy Options ($50+/ft)

When budget is less of a constraint and you're focused on aesthetics, sound blocking, or building something that lasts decades with zero maintenance, these premium options deliver.

Horizontal Plank Fencing

Horizontal fencing has become one of the most requested styles in Winnipeg's newer neighbourhoods. Instead of vertical pickets, wide planks (typically 2x6 or 1x6) run horizontally between posts, creating a modern, architectural look.

The style uses more lumber than vertical board fencing because the posts need to be closer together (typically 6 feet apart instead of 8) to prevent the horizontal boards from bowing under their own weight. The tighter post spacing and heavier boards bring the cost to $45-55 per foot installed in pressure-treated wood, and $55-65 per foot in cedar.

One thing to consider: horizontal boards can act as a ladder for children and pets. If you have small kids or dogs that climb, this may not be the ideal layout. Horizontal fences also tend to show warping more visibly than vertical fences, so using kiln-dried lumber and metal posts helps keep things straight over time.

Panel Fencing (Buy by the Panel)

Pre-built fence panels offer a consistent, factory-finished look that's hard to replicate with stick-built fencing. Instead of individual boards nailed to rails on-site, you're installing complete panels — typically 6 or 8 feet wide — between posts.

Panel fencing works well for homeowners who want a uniform appearance across a long run. The panels arrive pre-assembled with consistent board spacing, straight cuts, and often decorative top profiles (lattice, scalloped, or dog-ear). Premium panel systems in cedar, composite, or aluminum start around $50-70 per foot installed.

The downside is that panels don't accommodate uneven ground as easily as stick-built fencing. On sloped lots, panels must be stair-stepped (each panel level but offset vertically from the next), which creates triangular gaps at the bottom that may need fill boards.

Pergola and Privacy Fence Combinations

Combining a privacy fence with an overhead pergola structure creates a semi-enclosed outdoor room. This approach is popular for patio areas where you want both lateral privacy and partial shade or overhead coverage.

A pergola-fence combination typically involves a 6-foot privacy fence on two or three sides with a pergola frame overhead, sometimes with retractable shade fabric or climbing plants. The cost depends heavily on the size and materials, but expect $60-100 per linear foot for the combined structure.

This option works best for a defined area (a patio, hot tub surround, or seating area) rather than a full perimeter. The overhead structure adds visual weight and can make a small yard feel enclosed, so it's usually used selectively rather than wrapping an entire property.

Concrete and Masonry Walls

A concrete or masonry wall is the ultimate privacy barrier. Nothing blocks sightlines, sound, and wind more completely than a solid wall. They're common in commercial applications and are increasingly used by residential homeowners near highways, rail lines, or busy streets.

Costs range from $80-150 per foot depending on height, finish, and engineering requirements. A poured concrete wall requires proper footings below Winnipeg's frost line (roughly 4 feet deep), and the footings themselves add significant cost. Decorative concrete block walls (split-face, textured, or stucco-finished) cost less than poured concrete and offer more design flexibility.

Concrete walls are permanent and virtually maintenance-free. They don't rot, blow over, or degrade in any meaningful way. A well-built concrete wall will outlast the house behind it. The tradeoff is the upfront investment and the fact that removal (if you ever change your mind) is expensive and disruptive.

Soundproof Fencing

If your goal is noise reduction rather than just visual privacy, standard wood fences provide only modest help. A typical 6-foot wood privacy fence reduces noise by about 5-10 decibels — noticeable, but not transformative for homes facing heavy traffic or railway corridors.

Purpose-built acoustic fencing uses dense materials and engineered construction to block significantly more sound:

  • Acoustic fence panels — wood or composite panels with a mass-loaded vinyl (MLV) core. These reduce noise by 15-20 decibels, cutting perceived loudness roughly in half. Available as complete panel systems that install on standard steel posts.
  • Concrete sound walls — the gold standard for noise blocking. Precast concrete panels (the same type used along highways) reduce noise by 20-30 decibels. Residential versions are available in textured, stained, or stucco finishes that look far better than highway barriers.
  • Earth berms + fencing — a raised mound of soil topped with a fence combines mass (the earth) with height. This approach works well on larger properties where you have room for the berm's footprint.

Sound travels over and around barriers, not just through them. For acoustic fencing to work, it needs to be tall enough and long enough to block the direct path between the noise source and your living area. A short section of soundproof fencing with open ends won't accomplish much. An acoustics consultant can help you design an effective layout if noise reduction is the primary goal.

Can You Make an Existing Fence Taller?

Many homeowners with a 5-foot fence wonder if they can add height without replacing the whole thing. The short answer is yes, with some caveats.

Winnipeg fence bylaws limit fence height to 2.0 metres (about 6.5 feet) in rear and side yards. If your current fence is under that limit, you have room to add height. Common approaches include:

  • Lattice extensions — a 1-2 foot lattice panel mounted on top of the existing fence. This is the most common and affordable method. Lattice adds partial privacy (about 50-60%) and lets light and air through, which reduces wind load on the fence. Cost: $5-10 per foot for materials.
  • Post extensions — metal or wood brackets that bolt onto existing posts to extend them upward, then you attach new boards or panels to the extended section. This works best when the existing posts are in good condition. Cost: $10-18 per foot installed.
  • Trellis with climbing plants — a trellis mounted above the fence gives climbing plants a structure to grow on. Virginia creeper, hops, and certain clematis varieties do well in Winnipeg. This combines partial immediate privacy (from the trellis) with increasing privacy as the plants fill in.

Before adding height, check two things. First, make sure the existing posts can support the added wind load — taller fences catch more wind, and Winnipeg delivers plenty of it. Second, confirm that the total height stays within the bylaw limit. Fence heights are measured from the ground on the higher side of the fence, not from grade on your side.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to add privacy to an existing fence in Winnipeg?

Privacy slats for chain link are the cheapest option at $5-8 per foot for materials, and they're easy to install yourself. Fabric privacy screens are even cheaper at $3-5 per foot but are temporary. For wood fences with gaps, adding a second layer of boards (board-on-board conversion) runs $8-12/ft in materials.

Can I make my existing fence taller for more privacy?

Yes, but Winnipeg bylaws limit fence height to 2.0 metres (6.5 feet) in rear and side yards and 1.0 metre (3.3 feet) in front yards. You can add lattice, trellis panels, or post extensions to reach the maximum allowed height. The posts must be structurally capable of supporting the added height and wind load.

Is vinyl fencing good for Winnipeg winters?

Modern vinyl fencing is engineered to handle extreme cold. Quality vinyl won't crack, rot, or warp in Winnipeg's -35°C winters. The key is choosing commercial-grade vinyl with metal post reinforcements — not the thin, unreinforced panels sold at big-box stores. Properly installed vinyl fencing lasts 25-30 years in Manitoba with zero maintenance.

How effective is soundproof fencing at blocking traffic noise?

A solid wood privacy fence reduces noise by 5-10 decibels — noticeable but modest. Purpose-built acoustic fence panels with mass-loaded vinyl cores reduce noise by 15-20 decibels, cutting perceived loudness roughly in half. Concrete or masonry walls offer the best sound blocking at 20-30 decibels of reduction. For homes near highways or rail lines, a concrete wall or acoustic panel system is the most effective option.
