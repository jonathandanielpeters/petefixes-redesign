Fencing Guides

Every Fence Type Available in Winnipeg: The Complete Comparison Guide (2026)

April 5, 2026 · 14 min read

Winnipeg homeowners and businesses have more fence options than ever. From the classic chain link that lines every back lane in the city to modern vinyl panels and ornamental iron, the range of materials, styles, and price points can feel overwhelming when you're just trying to figure out what to put around your yard.

This guide covers every fence type we install at Pete Fixes — and a few we don't — so you can make an informed choice before you spend a dollar. We've rated each type for Winnipeg's specific conditions: frost heave, wind exposure, snow loads, and the reality of our short building season. If you're looking for pricing, our complete fence cost guide breaks down the numbers per foot.

Chain Link Fencing — the Winnipeg Workhorse

There's a reason chain link is everywhere in Winnipeg. It's affordable, it handles our winters without complaint, and it lasts decades with zero maintenance. If you've ever heard someone call it a "diamond fence" or "china link" — same product, different name. The diamond-shaped mesh pattern is what gives it that first nickname, and "china link" is a regional term you'll hear around Manitoba and Saskatchewan.

Residential Chain Link

For residential use, chain link fences typically run 4 to 6 feet tall with 11 or 11.5 gauge wire. You have three main finish options:

  • Galvanized (silver): The standard. Zinc-coated steel wire that resists rust and costs the least. It's not flashy, but it works.
  • Black vinyl-coated: The same galvanized wire with a PVC coating bonded over top. The black finish blends into landscaping far better than silver and adds an extra layer of corrosion protection. This is our most popular residential chain link option.
  • Privacy inserts: Vertical or diagonal slats woven through the chain link mesh to block sightlines. Available in grey, green, brown, or black. They turn an open chain link fence into a semi-private barrier without the cost of a full wood fence.

Commercial and Industrial Chain Link

Commercial chain link steps up in both height and gauge. Warehouses, storage yards, construction sites, and industrial properties typically use 8 to 12-foot chain link with 9 gauge wire — noticeably heavier than residential mesh. Add barbed wire or razor ribbon at the top and you have a serious security perimeter.

If you need to secure a commercial property, our fencing services page covers the full range of commercial and industrial options.

Baseball Diamond Fences and Backstops

Backstops and outfield fences are a specialty application of heavy-gauge chain link. Backstop fences are typically 12 to 20 feet tall with angled overhang sections, built on heavier posts and top rail to withstand repeated ball impacts. Community leagues, schools, and municipalities across Winnipeg use chain link backstops because the mesh absorbs ball impact, the sight lines stay open for spectators, and the material handles our winters without seasonal teardown.

Construction Fencing (Temporary Chain Link)

Temporary construction fencing is chain link mesh mounted in portable steel frames — typically 6-foot panels on weighted bases. Construction sites, road projects, and event perimeters in Winnipeg use these panels because they go up and come down in hours, require no permanent post installation, and can be repositioned as the project evolves. They're rented or purchased by the panel.

Wood Fencing — Why We Build Hybrid Only

Wood fencing is the default choice for privacy in Winnipeg. Solid boards block sightlines completely, and the natural look of wood suits everything from century-old Wolseley homes to new builds in Bridgwater. We install wood fencing in every style — but with one critical difference from most contractors: every wood fence we build uses metal posts.

Why Hybrid Construction (Metal Posts + Wood Boards)

Winnipeg's clay soil, deep frost line, and relentless freeze-thaw cycles are brutal on wood posts. A pressure-treated 4x4 set in concrete will start rotting at the ground line within 8 to 12 years. The concrete footing itself becomes a frost heave anchor — the soil grips the concrete and pushes the whole assembly upward, leaving you with leaning, heaving posts and a fence that looks tired well before it should.

Our approach: steel posts driven directly into the ground with hydraulic equipment, then wood boards and rails attached to the metal frame. No concrete, no rot, no heaving. The wood boards give you the look and privacy you want. The metal posts give you structural integrity that lasts 30+ years. For the full breakdown of why this matters, read our metal fence posts guide.

Wood Fence Styles

  • Vertical board (standard privacy): The most common style in Winnipeg. 1x6 boards installed vertically, side by side, with no gaps. Full privacy, clean appearance, and the lowest wood fence price point.
  • Horizontal plank: 2x6 boards run horizontally between posts. This is the modern, architectural look — clean lines, warm wood tones, and a more contemporary feel. It uses more material and costs more than vertical board.
  • Board-on-board: Boards overlap on alternating sides of the rail, so there are no gaps when viewed from either side. Both sides of the fence look "finished" — there's no ugly back side. This is worth the extra cost if you share a fence line with a neighbour and want the fence to look good from both yards.
  • Shadowbox: Similar to board-on-board but with deliberate spacing between boards. The offset pattern creates partial privacy while allowing airflow and dappled light through the fence. It reduces wind load (important in Winnipeg's open prairie wind) and uses less lumber than full board-on-board.

Wood Types: Pressure-Treated vs Cedar

Pressure-treated spruce or pine is the standard and most affordable option. The chemical treatment (typically MCA — micronized copper azole) protects against rot and insect damage. It arrives green-tinted and weathers to grey over 1-2 years unless stained.

Cedar is naturally rot-resistant without chemical treatment. It starts as a warm reddish-brown and ages to silver-grey if left unfinished. Cedar smells great, looks beautiful, and costs roughly 40% more than pressure-treated. It's the premium choice for homeowners who want a natural, high-end appearance.

Picket Fences

Picket fences are shorter (3 to 4 feet) decorative fences typically used in front yards. They mark a property boundary and add curb appeal without blocking sightlines. Traditional wood picket fences use pointed, rounded, or flat-top pickets spaced 2 to 3 inches apart. They're a classic look for older Winnipeg neighbourhoods — River Heights, Crescentwood, Wolseley — where heritage-style homes call for something more than chain link but less imposing than a privacy fence.

Vinyl Fencing

Vinyl (PVC) fencing is the zero-maintenance option. It never needs painting, staining, or sealing. It won't rot, warp, or splinter. A garden hose or pressure washer is the only upkeep it ever requires.

You may hear vinyl called "plastic fence," but modern vinyl fencing is engineered PVC reinforced with titanium dioxide for UV resistance — it's a far cry from the brittle, yellowing plastic panels of twenty years ago. Quality vinyl fencing holds its colour for decades and handles Winnipeg's temperature swings from +35°C to -40°C without cracking.

Vinyl Fence Styles

  • Privacy: Solid tongue-and-groove panels with no gaps. Available in white, tan, grey, and other colours. This is the most popular vinyl style — full privacy with a clean, uniform appearance.
  • Semi-privacy: Panels with deliberate spacing or lattice sections at the top. Allows some airflow and light while maintaining most of the privacy.
  • Picket: Open picket style, similar to a wood picket fence but with no maintenance. Popular for front yards and decorative boundaries.

How We Install Vinyl

Like our wood fences, we install vinyl panels on metal posts only. The vinyl panels slide into or attach to the steel post framework. This gives you the same frost-heave resistance and longevity as our wood fence installations. We do not offer vinyl on wood posts — it defeats the purpose of choosing a maintenance-free material if the posts rot out underneath it.

Vinyl vs Composite

Composite fencing is a different product. Where vinyl is pure PVC, composite fencing blends wood fibres with plastic polymers — think of it as the decking material (Trex, TimberTech) applied to fencing. Composite looks more like real wood than vinyl does, but it's heavier, more expensive, and still relatively uncommon in Winnipeg. We can source and install composite fencing on request, but vinyl and wood remain our most popular privacy fence materials by a wide margin.

Ornamental Metal Fencing

Ornamental metal fencing gives you the elegant look of wrought iron without the wrought iron price tag or maintenance burden. You'll hear this style called bar fence, wrought iron fence, iron fence, steel fence, or aluminum fence depending on who you're talking to. They all refer to the same general category: vertical metal pickets (bars) welded into horizontal rails, creating an open, decorative fence.

Aluminum vs Steel

The two main materials for ornamental fencing are aluminum and steel, and they serve different purposes:

  • Aluminum: Lightweight, rust-proof (aluminum doesn't rust — it oxidizes to form a protective layer), and available in a huge range of styles and colours. Powder-coated aluminum is the standard for residential ornamental fencing. It costs less to ship and install than steel, and it holds up well in Winnipeg's climate. The trade-off is that it's not as strong as steel — a hard impact can bend the pickets.
  • Steel: Heavier, stronger, and more resistant to physical damage. Steel ornamental fencing is powder-coated over galvanization (the same dual-layer protection as our steel fence posts). It's the choice for high-traffic areas, commercial properties, and anywhere the fence might take a hit from lawn equipment, vehicles, or weather debris. It costs more than aluminum.

Pool-Code Compliant Options

If you're fencing a pool, Winnipeg's building code requires self-closing, self-latching gates and specific picket spacing (no more than 4 inches between bars, no climbable horizontal rails). Ornamental aluminum and steel fences are available in pool-code compliant configurations — it's one of the most common applications for this fence type.

Powder-Coated Finishes

Ornamental fencing comes in a range of powder-coated colours, with black being the most popular by a wide margin. Bronze, dark brown, white, and hunter green are also available. The powder coat is baked on at high temperature, creating a hard, UV-resistant finish that won't peel or flake like paint.

Welded Wire Fencing

Welded wire fencing uses rigid mesh panels made from steel wire welded at every intersection, mounted on metal posts. It looks distinctly different from chain link — the grid pattern is clean and geometric rather than the woven diamond pattern of chain link.

Welded wire has a more modern, refined appearance. The mesh is typically powder-coated black or green, and the rigid panels stay flat and taut without the slight sag that chain link can develop between posts. It's a good middle ground between the affordability of chain link and the visual appeal of ornamental fencing.

Common Applications

  • Gardens and raised beds: Keeps animals out without blocking sunlight.
  • Property boundaries: Defines your lot line with a clean, modern look that's less institutional than chain link.
  • Dog enclosures: The rigid mesh has no flex, so dogs can't push through or create gaps.
  • Rural and acreage properties: Longer runs at a lower cost than ornamental or wood fencing.

Panel Fencing

Panel fencing refers to any fence system sold and installed as pre-built panels rather than individual components. Instead of building the fence board by board on site, the panels arrive assembled — you set the posts and attach the panels between them.

This category spans multiple materials. Vinyl fencing is almost always panel-based. Many ornamental fences come as pre-welded panels. Wood fence panels (pre-assembled sections of boards on rails) are available at most building supply stores for DIY projects, though professional installations typically build wood fences on site for better fit and quality.

Why Buy By the Panel?

  • Speed: Panel installation is significantly faster than stick-built fencing. The panels drop into place between posts, and you're done.
  • Consistency: Every panel is identical — same board spacing, same rail position, same overall dimensions. No variation from section to section.
  • Commercial applications: Large commercial sites often specify panel fencing because it can be installed quickly, replaced section by section if damaged, and sourced in bulk.

Specialty and Less Common Options

Indoor Fencing

Not every fence goes outside. Indoor fencing serves specific purposes in commercial and industrial settings: warehouse safety barriers separating pedestrian walkways from forklift traffic, machine guarding around industrial equipment, pet barriers in veterinary clinics and daycares, and crowd control systems in retail and event spaces. These are typically welded wire mesh or tubular steel panels bolted to the floor or weighted bases.

Living Fences (Hedges and Trees)

A row of cedars, caragana, or lilac bushes is the original privacy fence. Living fences are common in established Winnipeg neighbourhoods, especially where mature hedges have been growing for decades. They're beautiful when maintained, but they require ongoing trimming, take years to fill in, and can cause disputes with neighbours when roots or branches cross property lines. We don't install living fences, but we frequently install traditional fencing alongside or in place of aging hedges.

Concrete and Masonry Walls

Concrete block walls and stone walls are occasionally used in Winnipeg for retaining walls that double as property boundaries, or for high-end commercial frontages. They're expensive, require proper footings (which complicates things in our frost-heave-prone soil), and are outside our scope of work. For most residential applications in Winnipeg, a fence achieves the same goal at a fraction of the cost.

Composite Fencing in Detail

Composite fencing blends wood fibres (or wood flour) with high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or polypropylene plastic. The result is a board that looks and feels more like real wood than vinyl does, with much lower maintenance than natural wood. It won't rot, warp, or splinter, and it doesn't need staining.

The downsides: composite fencing costs significantly more than either wood or vinyl, it's heavier (which means stronger posts and more labour), and colour options are more limited than vinyl. It's gaining popularity in other parts of Canada but remains a niche product in Winnipeg. If you're interested, we can source composite panels and install them on our standard metal post system.

Metal vs Wood Posts — the Definitive Answer for Winnipeg

This question comes up on every fence project, so here's the short version: metal posts, always, in Winnipeg. The clay soil heaves. Wood rots. Concrete footings make heaving worse. Driven steel posts solve all three problems at a comparable installed cost.

We've written an entire article on this topic — why metal fence posts beat wood in Winnipeg — covering the driven post method, Postmaster posts, powder-coated 2x2 steel posts, and the real cost comparison. If you're debating between metal and wood posts, read that before you decide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best fence type for Winnipeg's climate?

Chain link and wood (on metal posts) are the most proven fence types for Winnipeg's freeze-thaw cycles, heavy snow loads, and high winds. Both handle Manitoba winters reliably when installed correctly. Vinyl and ornamental aluminum also perform well, though vinyl can become brittle in extreme cold if a lower-grade product is used. The "best" fence depends on your priorities — privacy, cost, appearance, or maintenance.

What is the cheapest fence to install in Winnipeg?

Galvanized chain link is the cheapest fence type to install in Winnipeg, starting around $18–$22 per foot for a 4-foot residential fence. For privacy fencing, a standard 6-foot pressure-treated wood fence at $28–$36 per foot is the most affordable option that blocks sightlines completely.

Should I use wood posts or metal posts for my fence in Winnipeg?

Metal posts are the better choice for Winnipeg. Wood posts rot at the ground line in 8–12 years, and concrete footings heave in Manitoba's clay soil. Metal posts are driven directly into the ground — no concrete, no rot, no heaving. The installed cost is comparable to wood posts set in concrete, but the posts last 30+ years. Read our full metal posts guide for the detailed comparison.

What is the lowest maintenance fence material?

Vinyl (PVC) fencing requires the least maintenance — it never needs painting, staining, or sealing, and cleans with a garden hose. Chain link and ornamental aluminum are close seconds. Wood fencing requires the most maintenance, needing staining or sealing every 2–3 years to maintain its appearance and prevent premature weathering.
