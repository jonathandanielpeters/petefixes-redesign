How Much Does a Fence Cost in Winnipeg? 2026 Price Guide

April 5, 2026 · 10 min read

Whether you're pricing out a wood privacy fence, a basic chain link fence, or an ornamental iron fence, the first question is always the same: how much will it cost? This guide breaks down real fence costs in Winnipeg for 2026, including materials, labour, and the factors that move the price up or down.

Fence Cost Per Foot in Winnipeg: Quick Reference

These are installed prices (materials + labour) for a standard 6-foot-tall fence in Winnipeg as of spring 2026:

How Much Is a Wood Fence in Winnipeg?

A standard 6-foot pressure-treated wood fence with 1x6 boards costs between $28 and $36 per foot installed in Winnipeg. This includes posts (typically on 8-foot centres), three horizontal rails, and dog-eared or flat-top pickets.

Factors that increase wood fence cost:

  • Cedar boards add $12–$15/ft over pressure-treated lumber. Cedar is naturally rot-resistant and ages beautifully, but costs more upfront
  • Horizontal plank style (using 2x6 boards) is a modern look that uses more material and requires different framing. Expect $38–$48/ft
  • Top cap rail (a 2x4 across the top) adds a finished look for about $2/ft
  • Metal posts (Postmaster or 2x2 steel) instead of wood 4x4 posts add $25–$30 per post but last much longer in Manitoba's freeze-thaw cycles
  • Board-on-board or shadowbox patterns use more lumber and cost $3–$5/ft more than standard

Cheapest Wood Fence Option

The most affordable privacy fence in Winnipeg is a standard 6-foot pressure-treated fence with 1x6 boards, wood posts, and 2 rails instead of 3. At roughly $26–$30/ft, it gives you full privacy at the lowest wood fence price point. It won't win design awards, but it gets the job done.

How Much Is a Chain Link Fence in Winnipeg?

Chain link remains the cheapest fencing option in Winnipeg for basic property division. A 6-foot galvanized chain link fence runs $26–$30/ft installed. Here's how options affect the price:

  • Galvanized (silver) is the baseline — functional, durable, and affordable
  • Black vinyl coating looks cleaner and resists rust better, adding $3–$5/ft
  • Privacy inserts (slats woven through the chain link) add $4–$6/ft and come in grey, green, brown, or black. They're a cost-effective way to add privacy to chain link without building a wood fence
  • Shorter fences (4 or 5 feet) cost less — roughly $18–$24/ft

For a perimeter fence for a dog or basic yard boundary, a 4-foot chain link fence at around $18–$22/ft is hard to beat on price.

How to Calculate Your Fence Cost

To estimate your total fence cost in Winnipeg, you need three numbers:

  1. Total linear footage: Measure or pace the perimeter where you want the fence. A typical Winnipeg lot might have 150–200 feet of fence-able perimeter.
  2. Cost per foot: Use the table above based on your fence type and material choices.
  3. Gates: Pedestrian gates typically add $250–$500 each. Double drive gates (for vehicles) add $600–$1,200 each.

Example: 160 feet of 6' pressure-treated wood fence + 1 pedestrian gate + 1 double drive gate:

  • Fence: 160 ft × $32/ft = $5,120
  • Pedestrian gate: $350
  • Double drive gate: $850
  • Total estimate: $6,320

What Affects Fence Pricing in Winnipeg?

Material Costs and Supply

Lumber and steel prices fluctuate. Pressure-treated wood prices have stabilized since the 2021-2022 spikes, but cedar pricing remains elevated. We recommend getting quotes early in the season (March-April) before the summer rush drives up both material costs and wait times.

Fence Height

A 4-foot fence uses significantly less material than a 6-foot fence. Dropping from 6' to 5' can save 10-15% on materials.

Terrain and Access

Sloped yards, rocky soil, or limited access (no back lane, tight side yards) can increase labour costs. Winnipeg's gumbo clay also makes post-hole digging harder than sandy or loam soils, though most professional installers account for this in their standard pricing.

Post Type

Wood 4x4 posts are cheapest upfront but rot in 8–12 years in Manitoba's climate. Metal posts (Postmaster, steel 2x2) cost more but last 25+ years. For a fence you won't have to rebuild in a decade, metal posts are worth the investment.

Season and Timing

June through August is peak season for fence installation in Winnipeg. Booking in spring or fall can sometimes mean shorter wait times. Most fence contractors are booking 4-6 weeks out during peak season.

Commercial and Industrial Fencing Costs

Commercial chain link fencing in Winnipeg (8-foot, heavy gauge) runs $35–$50/ft. Industrial fencing for warehouses, construction sites, and storage yards often requires barbed wire or razor ribbon topping, adding $3–$8/ft. Bollard installations for parking lot perimeters start at $300–$500 per bollard, installed.

Specialty applications like garbage enclosures, baseball diamond fences (backstops), and parking fencing are quoted on a project basis. Contact us for commercial estimates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to fence a typical Winnipeg backyard?

A standard Winnipeg lot with approximately 150 linear feet of fencing costs between $4,200 and $6,000 for a 6-foot pressure-treated wood fence, including one gate. Cedar or ornamental options will be higher.

Is it cheaper to build a fence myself?

DIY saves on labour (roughly 40% of total cost), but you'll need to rent equipment, handle post alignment, and ensure the fence meets bylaws. Mistakes in post spacing or depth are expensive to fix. If your time is valuable, professional installation pays for itself in speed and warranty coverage.

What's the cheapest fence for dogs in Winnipeg?

A 4-foot galvanized chain link fence at $18–$22/ft is the most affordable option for containing dogs. For larger or more athletic breeds, go with 5 or 6 feet.

How much do privacy fence inserts cost?

Privacy slats for chain link fencing run $4–$6/ft installed. For a DIY approach, you can buy inserts at local fence supply stores for $2–$3/ft and install them yourself in an afternoon.
